hi there, I'm

Dianna Radpour

I'm a third-year PhD student in Information Science (joint degree in Cognitive Science) at the University of Colorado Boulder, advised by Martha Palmer. My research interests focus on computational semantics, ontology design, and social informatics.

In my free time, I enjoy painting, biking, exploring (occasionally writing about) new avenues for culinary creativity, and skating (ice/inline).

Research

Research Interen
Riken Center for Advanced Intelligence Project
  • Spent a semester in Tokyo, Japan interning with the Language Information Access Technology team.
  • Researched and developed computational models for unsupervised learning and automatic suggestion of attribute candidates from unstructured text corpora to be verified in large-scale ontology development
  • PI: Satoshi Sekine
JournyX

  • Researched free PDU websites to advertise companys webinars through project management forums
University of Colorado Boulder
Research Intern
  • Research and development of computational models to aid in the discovery of salient segments of text through integration of NLP methods guided by domain knowledge and user-feedback
  • Implementing techniques spanning from traditional to interactive machine learning for user-centric qualitative data analysis for NSF-funded Cyber-Human Systems research (scaling qualitative inductive analysis through computational methods).
  • PI: Danielle Szafir
